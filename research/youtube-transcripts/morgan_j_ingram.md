# morgan j ingram: Sourcing $2M in Pipeline as an SDR

## Executive Summary

This document outlines the five-step outbound infrastructure used to scale individual pipeline generation from zero to $2,000,000. It covers the transition from unorganized, low-yield outreach to a structured framework balancing high-volume experimentation, strict calendar management, and channel optimization. This framework serves as an operational blueprint for SDRs, Account Executives, and outbound growth engineers looking to build scalable pipeline systems.

---

## Main Ideas & Themes

- **Continuous Knowledge Sourcing:** Treating the outbound role as an optimization problem—consuming industry data daily and treating top performers as repositories of system secrets.
- **The Stoplight Framework:** Shifting from sporadic, high-variance scheduling to rigid time-blocking categorized by priority vectors.
- **Persuasion as a Volume Game:** Treating conversational flow as a trainable skill refined through reps, rather than an unchangeable personality trait.
- **The Steph Curry Principle (Channel-Market Fit):** Identifying a primary high-converting outreach mechanism and using secondary channels strictly as routing systems back to the main node.
- **High-Yield Tracking Systems:** Pruning operational metrics down to the core indicators that directly impact revenue velocity.

---



## Key Takeaways

- **De-risk by Interviewing Outliers:** Do not build strategies in a silo. Interview top-performing reps to extract working edge-cases, system configurations, and historical failures.
- **Deep Buyer Mapping:** Audit actual customer transcripts and sit on Customer Success calls to parse the exact nomenclature and pain points of the ideal customer profile (ICP).
- **Calendar Discipline Dictates Yield:** Sporadic inputs generate sporadic outputs. Protect high-conversion calling windows (e.g., morning/afternoon pick-up spikes) from internal meetings.
- **Parse Irregular Node Responses:** Listen for qualitative friction words in conversations (e.g., "It's fine," or "We're doing okay"). Treat these as triggers to execute deep-dive debugging questions.
- **High-Volume Prototyping:** Run high-volume tests across multiple protocols (Email, LinkedIn, Calls, Video) early on to identify where your personal conversion rate peaks.

## Actionable Insights (Implementation Guide)



### 1. Configure the Stoplight Calendar Method

Partition your daily routine into three isolated operational blocks:

- **Green Blocks (Money-Making Activities):** High-focus periods reserved strictly for outward inputs (Cold calling, video engineering, custom LinkedIn sequencing). No internal meetings allowed.
- **Yellow Blocks (System Preparation):** Time dedicated to pipeline prep, target research, system optimization, and technical upskilling.
- **Red Blocks (Rest/Reset):** Compulsory 15–30 minute buffers scheduled every 90 minutes to mitigate cognitive decline and burnout.



### 2. Implement the Steph Curry Routing Protocol

Identify your highest-converting pipeline channel and focus your core bandwidth there. 

- *Example Execution:* If personalized video generates your highest conversion rate, run an aggressive baseline of 20 high-quality videos/day. 
- Configure your secondary layers (cold calls, automated emails) to act solely as routing pointers to drive target attention back to that primary channel.



### 3. Track the "Big Three" Core Metrics

Deprecate vanity metrics like overall open rates or negative response percentages. Maintain a lean spreadsheet or telemetry dashboard tracking only these three endpoints:

1. **Positive Reply Rate (PRR):** Total volume of intent-positive interactions.
2. **Pipeline Dollar Attribution:** Total closed/won and active pipeline pipeline value tied directly to your source key.
3. **Weekly Quality Meetings (WQM):** High-intent meetings successfully handed off to AEs.



### 4. Execute a Peer Secrets Audit

Schedule brief syncs with the top 3 output producers in your org. Run an audit using these exact prompts:

- "What specific workflow or script modification is yielding the highest conversion for you this week?"
- "What traditional sequence plays have you deprecated due to low efficiency?"
- "If you had to reset your target accounts today, how would you restructure your baseline sequencing?"
