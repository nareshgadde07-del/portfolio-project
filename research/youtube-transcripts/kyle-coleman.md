# Kyle Coleman — Cold Email Mistakes Every Sales Rep Should Avoid

## Executive Summary

In this Lavender Live session, Kyle Coleman shares the most common mistakes sales professionals make in cold email outreach and explains how modern buyer behavior has changed. His advice centers on writing shorter, simpler, more human emails that educate prospects instead of immediately pushing for meetings.

# Key Takeaways

- Keep cold emails between **50–75 words** whenever possible.
- Every email should focus on **one problem or one value proposition**, not multiple.
- Spread additional value across follow-up emails rather than cramming everything into the first message.
- Most cold emails are still too long, overly formal, and difficult to read.
- Use **white space** to improve readability by separating different ideas into short paragraphs.
- Avoid copying website marketing copy directly into outbound emails.
- Cold prospects need education—not product marketing language.
- Use **simple, conversational words** instead of complex vocabulary.
  - Say **"use"** instead of **"utilize."**
- Aim for approximately a **5th–8th grade reading level** to maximize clarity.
- Effective communication principles apply across:
  - Cold emails
  - Cold calls
  - Internal communication
  - Slack messages
  - Customer conversations
- Consider your audience's communication style.
  - Younger buyers often prefer concise, text-message-style communication.
  - Some senior executives may expect slightly more formal introductions.
- Modern buyers have access to product reviews, demos, and educational content before speaking with sales.
- Because buyers are more informed today, sales emails should educate rather than sell immediately.
- Replace aggressive meeting requests with curiosity-driven calls to action.
- Ask whether someone is interested in learning more instead of immediately requesting calendar time.
- Research shows most buyers are **indecisive**, making confidence-heavy messaging less effective.
- Use language that reduces buyer pressure rather than increasing it.
- Sales success comes from nurturing relationships, not forcing immediate meetings.
- Consistent prospect education builds stronger pipeline over time.
- Excessive automation damages:
  - Email deliverability
  - Prospect relationships
  - Personalization quality
- Good personalization combines:
  - Persona insights
  - Company context
  - Individual-specific research

# Actionable Insights

### 1. Reduce Email Length

Target **50–75 words** for first-touch cold emails.

---

### 2. Focus on One Message

Instead of listing multiple product benefits:

- Email 1 → One problem
- Email 2 → Another insight
- Email 3 → Different value
- Continue educating throughout the sequence.

---

### 3. Improve Formatting

Write emails that are easy to scan.

Use:

- Short paragraphs
- White space
- Separate ideas clearly

Avoid giant walls of text.

---

### 4. Simplify Your Language

Replace corporate vocabulary with everyday words.

Examples:

- Utilize → Use
- Leverage → Help
- Facilitate → Make
- Optimize → Improve

Simple language improves comprehension and reply rates.

---

### 5. Stop Copying Marketing Copy

Website messaging targets interested buyers.

Cold emails target people who have shown **no interest yet**.

Rewrite messaging specifically for outbound prospecting.

---

### 6. Ask for Interest, Not Meetings

Instead of:

> "Do you have 30 minutes next Tuesday?"

Try:

> "Would it be worth sharing a few ideas?"

or

> "Would learning more be helpful?"

Reduce commitment before asking for calendar time.

---

### 7. Educate Before Selling

Modern buyers expect value before conversations.

Share:

- Insights
- Industry observations
- Useful ideas
- Relevant problems

Earn the meeting instead of demanding it.

---

### 8. Balance Automation with Personalization

Automation should increase efficiency—not eliminate relevance.

Combine:

- Buyer persona knowledge
- Company research
- Individual context

to create emails that feel genuinely personalized.

---

### 9. Build Pipeline with Long-Term Thinking

Don't optimize only for today's meeting count.

Consistent education and relationship building create stronger:

- Pipeline
- Reply rates
- Conversion rates
- Revenue

over time.
