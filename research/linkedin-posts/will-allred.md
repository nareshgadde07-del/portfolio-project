# Post 1 – Open Source AI Isn't Automatically the Right Choice		
		
**Topic:** AI & Enterprise Adoption		
		
## Summary		
		
William discusses the growing conversation around open-source AI following Palantir and NVIDIA's enterprise partnership. He questions whether companies should trust frontier AI providers with proprietary business data, explores concerns around protecting intellectual property, and asks whether open-source models are finally becoming enterprise-ready.		
		
## Main Takeaways		
		
- AI adoption is about more than model quality.		
- Data ownership and intellectual property are major enterprise concerns.		
- Open-source models provide organizations with greater control.		
- Businesses should evaluate tradeoffs instead of following AI hype.

# Post 2 – Followers Don't Matter More Than Life		
		
**Topic:** Founder Mindset		
		
## Summary		
		
After taking his first break from LinkedIn posting since 2020, William reflects on spending time with family, attending a close friend's wedding, getting back into running, and making memories with his child. Despite reaching 90,000 LinkedIn followers during the break, he emphasizes that meaningful life experiences outweigh social media growth.		
		
## Main Takeaways		
		
- Personal fulfillment is more important than vanity metrics.		
- Time away improves long-term perspective.		
- Family and health deserve equal attention alongside business.		
- Social media growth should never become the primary goal.

# Post 3 – The Internet Isn't Real Life		
		
**Topic:** Internet Culture & Marketing		
		
## Summary		
		
William explains the meaning of the term "normie" and highlights how spending excessive time in niche online communities can distort perspective. He reminds founders and marketers that most customers are ordinary people, not deeply immersed in internet culture.		
		
## Main Takeaways		
		
- Internet communities often exaggerate niche conversations.		
- Most buyers are ordinary people.		
- Products should be built for customers, not online communities.		
- Stay connected to real-world customer behavior.

# Post 4 – Why I Started Charging for My Cold Email Course		
		
**Topic:** Sales Education		
		
## Summary		
		
William shares why he stopped offering his cold email courses for free after discovering that very few learners completed them. Although enrollment numbers were high, completion rates remained extremely low. Charging a small amount created commitment and encouraged people to finish the training.		
		
## Main Takeaways		
		
- Free products are often undervalued.		
- Commitment increases when customers invest financially.		
- Completion rate matters more than enrollment.		
- Education should optimize for outcomes instead of signups.

# Post 5 – Startup Pace Is About Direction, Not Speed		
		
**Topic:** Founder Leadership		
		
## Summary		
		
Drawing from conversations with another founder, William explains that startup success depends on thoughtful execution rather than constant activity. Founders should validate markets, reflect before scaling, and avoid distractions that waste limited time and energy.		
		
## Main Takeaways		
		
- Reflection is a critical part of execution.		
- Validate markets before hiring or scaling.		
- Avoid unnecessary "side quests."		
- Sustainable execution beats constant hustle.

# Post 6 – Most Prospecting Misses the Buyer's Reality		
		
**Topic:** Prospecting & Demand Generation		
		
## Summary		
		
William argues that most sales outreach targets buyers who are already searching for a solution. Instead, effective prospecting creates demand by addressing customer pain points before introducing the product. Successful messaging begins with the buyer's perspective rather than the seller's features.		
		
## Main Takeaways		
		
- Buyers are not thinking about your product.		
- Pain-first messaging creates stronger conversations.		
- Demand creation expands pipeline beyond active buyers.		
- Focus on customer problems instead of product features.
