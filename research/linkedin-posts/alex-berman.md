# Alex Berman – LinkedIn Research

**LinkedIn Profile:** [https://www.linkedin.com/in/alexanderberman](https://www.linkedin.com/in/alexanderberman)

## Overview

Alex Berman is a B2B growth marketing leader, entrepreneur, and author. His recent LinkedIn content focuses on AI monetization, SaaS pricing models, competitive advantages (moats), and enterprise growth strategies.

---

# Post 1 – The Shift from Per-Seat Pricing to Outcome-Based Pricing

**Topic:** AI Pricing Models

### Summary

Alex explains that traditional per-user SaaS pricing becomes less effective as AI agents perform work independently of human users. He uses Intercom's AI support agent (Fin) as an example of charging customers based on successful outcomes instead of seats.

### Key Takeaways

- AI changes traditional SaaS pricing.
- Outcome-based pricing aligns vendor success with customer success.
- Usage-based pricing can improve customer retention and revenue growth.

---



# Post 2 – Your Moat Doesn't Exist Yet

**Topic:** Competitive Advantage

### Summary

Alex argues that most startups do not begin with a strong competitive moat. Instead, lasting advantages are built over time through customer relationships, proprietary data, embedded workflows, and specialization.

He highlights Veeva as an example of a company that earned its market position by focusing deeply on one industry.

### Key Takeaways

- Moats are built over time.
- Vertical specialization creates defensible businesses.
- Proprietary data and workflows are difficult for competitors to copy.

# Post 3 – Databricks Changed AI Pricing

**Topic:** AI Monetization

### Summary

Alex compares Databricks and Snowflake, explaining how Databricks accelerated growth by charging customers based on AI workloads instead of only traditional cloud usage.

### Key Takeaways

- AI workloads create new revenue opportunities.
- Pay-per-use pricing scales with customer value.
- AI usage is becoming a major SaaS revenue driver.

# Post 4 – Why Chinese Open-Source AI Models Are Winning

**Topic:** AI Industry Trends

### Summary

Alex discusses the growing adoption of Chinese open-source AI models such as DeepSeek and Qwen. He contrasts open-source and closed-source strategies and explains why companies like Meta have embraced open models.

### Key Takeaways

- Open-source AI adoption is increasing rapidly.
- Chinese AI models are becoming widely used.
- Business models influence whether companies open-source their technology.

---



# Post 5 – AI Costs Are Rising Despite Lower Model Prices

**Topic:** AI Economics

### Summary

Alex explains that although AI models are becoming cheaper per individual request, overall AI spending is increasing because companies are running more complex AI workflows and agents.

### Key Takeaways

- AI infrastructure costs remain significant.
- Multi-step AI agents consume substantially more compute.
- Companies need sustainable pricing models to remain profitable.
