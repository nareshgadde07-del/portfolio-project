# Post 1 – Deal Size Determines Whether LinkedIn Ads Work

**Topic:** LinkedIn Advertising Strategy

### Summary

Justin explains that many marketers blame LinkedIn Ads for being expensive when the real issue is deal size. High-value enterprise deals can justify higher advertising costs, while lower-value products often cannot support LinkedIn's premium CPCs.

### Main Takeaways

- Evaluate LinkedIn Ads based on deal value.
- Customer acquisition cost must match revenue potential.
- Forecast ROI before launching campaigns.

---

# Post 2 – Stop Measuring Marketing by Channel

**Topic:** Account-Based Marketing

### Summary

Justin argues that modern B2B buyers interact across multiple touchpoints—not just one marketing channel. By combining LinkedIn Ads, organic engagement, website visitor data, Sales Navigator signals, and CRM activity, marketers gain a complete picture of buying behavior.

### Main Takeaways

- Buyers interact through multiple channels.
- Measure activity at the account level instead of by channel.
- Different stakeholders engage at different stages of the buying journey.

# Post 3 – Proving LinkedIn's Impact on Revenue

**Topic:** Marketing Attribution

### Summary

Justin introduces DemandSense's Influenced Revenue feature, which connects LinkedIn ad exposure directly to pipeline and closed deals. This helps marketing teams demonstrate ROI with concrete evidence instead of relying on assumptions.

### Main Takeaways

- Connect advertising directly to closed revenue.
- Attribution improves executive confidence.
- Data-driven reporting strengthens marketing decisions.

# Post 4 – Building Smarter LinkedIn Ad Campaigns

**Topic:** Audience Strategy

### Summary

Justin emphasizes that successful LinkedIn advertising begins with warm audiences before expanding to colder prospects. He explains that understanding actual buyers, sequencing audiences correctly, and increasing relevant exposure leads to stronger campaign performance.

### Main Takeaways

- Prioritize warm audiences before cold outreach.
- Focus on real buyers rather than generic personas.
- Audience sequencing improves advertising efficiency.

# Post 5 – Website Behavior Completes the Attribution Story

**Topic:** Revenue Attribution

### Summary

Justin explains that traditional attribution models often miss critical buying behavior occurring on company websites. By combining website visits, ad impressions, CRM data, and customer interactions, businesses can better understand how marketing influences purchasing decisions.

### Main Takeaways

- Website behavior provides valuable buying signals.
- Attribution should include customer website activity.
- Complete customer journeys improve marketing ROI analysis.
